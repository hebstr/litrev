# Introduction

Les infiltrations de corticosteroïdes sous-acromiales (CSI) sont largement utilisées dans la prise en charge des douleurs d'épaule.

# Results

## CSI Versus Physiotherapy and Exercise

The GRASP trial, a large multicentre RCT (n=708), found no clinically significant benefit of corticosteroid injection over placebo at 12 months, with a mean difference in SPADI score of -2.17 (95% CI -4.70 to 0.35) [@Hopewell_2021a]. A Cochrane review of 39 trials confirmed that subacromial corticosteroid injection provides short-term pain relief (up to 8 weeks) but no long-term benefit over placebo [@Surace_2020].

## Systematic Review Evidence

A network meta-analysis including 47 studies found exercise therapy to be the most effective intervention for rotator cuff disease [@Page_2014]. A Cochrane overview of 25 systematic reviews concluded that the evidence for most interventions remains low to very low certainty [@Karjalainen_2019].

## Surgical Outcomes

The CSAW trial demonstrated that arthroscopic subacromial decompression was not superior to placebo surgery at 6 and 12 months [@Beard_2018].

# Discussion

## Main Findings

CSI provide short-term symptom relief but lack evidence for sustained benefit beyond 8 weeks [@Surace_2020; @Hopewell_2021a].

## Strengths and limitations of the review itself

- **Required limitation items**:
  1. This review was conducted with AI-assisted tools on 2026-04-02, with human verification at each phase gate.
  2. Citation chaining (snowballing) was not performed.
  3. Grey literature and institutional guidelines were not systematically searched.
