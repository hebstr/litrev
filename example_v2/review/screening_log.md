## Limitations

Screening performed by a single AI reviewer (LLM) using automated keyword-based classification. Several important caveats:

1. **PRISMA recommends dual independent screening.** For formal systematic reviews, a human second reviewer should validate a sample of decisions, particularly exclusions.
2. **Automated screening limitations.** The exclusion of "exclusively interventional" studies relied on keyword patterns in titles and abstracts. Some studies with secondary epidemiological outcomes may have been incorrectly excluded, and some studies without true epidemiological focus may have been incorrectly retained.
3. **Large retained set.** With 1114 articles retained, the set likely contains false positives (studies that do not truly report epidemiological outcomes). This is an expected trade-off for sensitivity-oriented screening. The extraction phase (litrev-extract) will apply a final filter when extracting claims.
4. **No-abstract articles.** 14 articles without abstracts were retained by default and should be assessed at the extraction stage when full text is available.
5. **Scoping review scope.** Full-text screening was limited to no-abstract articles only, per PRISMA-ScR guidelines for scoping reviews.
