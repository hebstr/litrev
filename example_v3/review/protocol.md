# Protocol — Subacromial Corticosteroid Injections for Shoulder Pain

## Research Question

What is the current evidence on subacromial corticosteroid injections in the management of shoulder pain in adults, regarding efficacy, predictive factors of response, place in the care pathway, and impact on surgical recourse?

## Framework: PICO

- **Population**: Adults (>=18 years) with shoulder pain (scapulalgia) or rotator cuff pathology
- **Intervention**: Subacromial corticosteroid injections (ultrasound-guided or landmark-guided)
- **Comparator**: Conservative treatment (physiotherapy, analgesics), placebo, or direct surgery
- **Outcomes**: (1) Efficacy (pain VAS/NRS, function scores, satisfaction); (2) Predictive factors of response or failure; (3) Place in the care sequence (delay before surgery, number of injections); (4) Complications and tendon effects

## Review Type

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR guideline)

State of the art for the injection component of the PRISE protocol, CHU de Lille.

## Period

2010-2026

## Geographic Priority

French data prioritized in synthesis; international data included for completeness.

## Databases

- PubMed/MEDLINE
- Semantic Scholar
- OpenAlex

## Search Concepts and Synonyms

1. **Shoulder pathology**: shoulder pain, scapulalgia, rotator cuff, supraspinatus, impingement syndrome, subacromial impingement, subacromial pain syndrome, tendinopathy shoulder
2. **Corticosteroid injection**: corticosteroid injection, steroid injection, cortisone injection, subacromial injection, glenohumeral injection, ultrasound-guided injection, image-guided injection
3. **Outcomes / care pathway**: efficacy, pain relief, functional outcome, predictive factors, prognostic factors, care pathway, surgical delay, time to surgery, tendon damage, complications

## Inclusion Criteria

- Adults >=18 years
- Subacromial or periarticular corticosteroid injections for shoulder pain / rotator cuff pathology
- Study designs: RCTs, cohort studies, case-control, cross-sectional, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, clinical guidelines, observational studies
- Published 2010-2026
- Language: English or French

## Exclusion Criteria

- Pediatric populations (<18 years)
- Tumor pathologies (except enchondroma if incidental context)
- Non-corticosteroid injections (PRP, hyaluronic acid, stem cells) as primary intervention (acceptable as comparator)
- Case reports, editorials, conference abstracts only
- Animal studies
