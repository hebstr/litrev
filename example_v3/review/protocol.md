# Protocol — Subacromial Corticosteroid Injections for Shoulder Pain

## Research Question

What is the current evidence on subacromial corticosteroid injections in the management of shoulder pain (scapulalgia) in adults, regarding efficacy, predictive factors of response, place in the care pathway, and impact on surgical outcomes?

## Framework: PICO

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Population** | Adults (≥18 years) with shoulder pain (scapulalgia) or rotator cuff pathology |
| **Intervention** | Subacromial corticosteroid injections (ultrasound-guided or landmark-guided) |
| **Comparison** | Conservative treatment (physiotherapy, analgesics), placebo (saline), or direct surgery |
| **Outcomes** | (1) Efficacy: pain (VAS/NRS), function (Constant, DASH, ASES), satisfaction; (2) Predictive factors of response or failure; (3) Place in care sequence: delay before surgery, number of injections, stepped-care positioning; (4) Complications and tendon effects |

## Review Type

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR guidelines).
Context: evidence mapping for the infiltration component of the PRISE protocol (CHU de Lille).

## Time Period

2010–2026

## Geographic Scope

French data prioritized in synthesis; international data included for completeness.

## Databases

1. PubMed/MEDLINE
2. Semantic Scholar
3. OpenAlex

## Boolean Strategy

(Concept 1) AND (Concept 2) AND (Concept 3 OR Concept 4)

## Inclusion Criteria

- Adults ≥18 years
- Subacromial corticosteroid injections (any technique)
- Reports at least one outcome: efficacy, predictive factors, care pathway positioning, complications, or surgical impact
- Published 2010–2026
- English or French language
- Study types: RCTs, cohort studies, case-control studies, cross-sectional studies, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, clinical guidelines, registry studies

## Exclusion Criteria

- Pediatric populations (<18 years)
- Tumoral pathologies (primary shoulder tumors)
- Non-corticosteroid injections as sole intervention (PRP, hyaluronic acid, viscosupplementation) — allowed as comparator arm
- Case reports, editorials, letters, conference abstracts without full text
- Animal or cadaveric studies
- Injections at sites other than subacromial space (glenohumeral, acromioclavicular) unless part of a multi-site protocol including subacromial
