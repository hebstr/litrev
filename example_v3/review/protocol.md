# Protocol — Subacromial Corticosteroid Injections for Shoulder Pain

## Research Question

What is the current evidence on subacromial corticosteroid injections for shoulder pain in adults regarding efficacy, predictive factors of response, place in the care pathway, and impact on surgical referral?

## Framework: PICO

- **Population**: Adults (>=18 years) with shoulder pain (scapulalgia) or rotator cuff pathology
- **Intervention**: Subacromial corticosteroid injections (ultrasound-guided or landmark-guided)
- **Comparator**: Conservative treatment (physiotherapy, analgesics), placebo, or direct surgery
- **Outcomes**: (1) Efficacy (pain VAS/NRS, function, satisfaction), (2) Predictive factors of response/failure, (3) Place in care sequence (delay before surgery, number of injections), (4) Complications and tendon effects

## Review Type

Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR guideline)

## Context

State of the art for the corticosteroid injections component of the PRISE protocol (PRatiques, Infiltrations et Suivi des douleurs d'Epaules), CHU de Lille.

## Time Period

2010–2026

## Geographic Priority

French data prioritized in synthesis; international data included for completeness.

## Inclusion Criteria

- Adults >=18 years
- Shoulder pain or rotator cuff pathology
- Subacromial corticosteroid injections (any guidance technique)
- Published 2010–2026
- English or French language
- Study types: RCTs, cohort studies, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, clinical guidelines, observational studies

## Exclusion Criteria

- Pediatric populations (<18 years)
- Tumor pathologies
- Non-corticosteroid injections (PRP, hyaluronic acid) as primary intervention (acceptable as comparator)
- Case reports with N<5
- Animal studies
