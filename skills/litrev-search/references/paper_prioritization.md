# Prioritizing High-Impact Papers

**Always prioritize influential, highly-cited papers from reputable authors and top venues.** Quality matters more than quantity in literature reviews.

> **Important caveats:** The thresholds, tiers, and heuristics below are rough starting points oriented toward biomedical and clinical research. They carry inherent biases toward English-language, Western, and high-income-country publications. Adjust them to your field, research question, and geographic scope. Lower-impact or non-English journals may publish essential regional evidence (e.g., tropical medicine, public health in LMICs). Always apply domain judgement rather than treating these numbers as rigid cutoffs.

## Citation Count Thresholds

Use citation counts as a rough signal — they vary widely by field, paper age, and database coverage:

| Paper Age | Citation Threshold | Classification |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| 0-3 years | 20+ citations | Noteworthy |
| 0-3 years | 100+ citations | Highly Influential |
| 3-7 years | 100+ citations | Significant |
| 3-7 years | 500+ citations | Landmark Paper |
| 7+ years | 500+ citations | Seminal Work |
| 7+ years | 1000+ citations | Foundational |

## Journal and Venue Tiers

Prioritize papers from higher-tier venues. Note: impact factor thresholds differ substantially across disciplines (e.g., IF 5 is high in mathematics but low in oncology). Adapt tiers to your specific field:

- **Tier 1 (Always Prefer):** NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, Annals of Internal Medicine, Nature Medicine, Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews
- **Tier 2 (Strong Preference):** Specialty leaders (Circulation, JCO, Gut, European Heart Journal, Diabetes Care, Chest, Thorax, IF>10)
- **Tier 3 (Include When Relevant):** Respected specialty journals (IF 5-10)
- **Tier 4 (Use Sparingly):** Lower-impact peer-reviewed venues — but may contain critical regional or niche evidence

## Author Reputation Assessment

These are soft signals, not hard filters. h-index thresholds vary by career stage, field size, and database coverage. Do not exclude relevant work solely based on author metrics:

- **Senior researchers** with high h-index (field-dependent; >40 is common in large biomedical fields but unrealistic in smaller disciplines)
- **Leading research groups** at recognized institutions — including strong regional and specialty centres, not only well-known Western universities
- **Authors with multiple Tier-1 publications** in the relevant field
- **Researchers with recognized expertise** (awards, editorial positions, society fellows)

## Identifying Seminal Papers

For any topic, identify foundational work by combining multiple signals (no single criterion is sufficient):
1. **High citation count** (field-dependent; 500+ for large biomedical fields, lower thresholds for smaller disciplines)
2. **Frequently cited by other included studies** (appears in many reference lists)
3. **Published in leading venues for the field** (not limited to Nature/Science/Cell — field-specific top journals count equally)
4. **Written by field pioneers** (often cited as establishing concepts)
