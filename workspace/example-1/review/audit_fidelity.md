# Fidelity Audit — Claims vs Sources

## Summary

- **1265 quantitative claims** extracted from **80 articles**
- Automated audit: 1 VERIFIED, 65 UNVERIFIED, 0 NO_ABSTRACT, 0 NO_EXTRACTION (66 claims with citations audited)
- BibTeX entries: 77 total in references.bib, 16 orphans (present in bib but never cited in review body)
- 55 unique citation keys used in review body; all 55 resolve to a bib entry (including 5 with special characters)

## Findings

### [F-FID-01] 16 orphan BibTeX entries — Minor

**Detail**: The following 16 keys are present in `references.bib` but never cited in the review body text: Andersen_2011, Cook_2021, Darbandi_2024, Gane_2017, Kunutsor_2020, Lu_2023, Matsudaira_2011, Maunder_2025, Mishra_2024, Parada_2021, Sarasua_2021, Tate_2012, Tegern_2020, Van_2021, Yue_2012, and the `Speklé_2010` key from the embedded BibTeX block.

These entries were generated by the bibliography tool for included articles but are not referenced in the synthesis text.

**Recommendation**: Remove orphan entries from `references.bib` to keep the bibliography consistent with the review body, or add citations where relevant content exists in the synthesis.

---

### [F-FID-02] High UNVERIFIED rate in claims audit — Minor

**Detail**: 65 of 66 audited numerical claims are UNVERIFIED, meaning the number associated with a citation in the review text was not found in the abstract-extracted claims for that article. This is expected for a scoping review where synthesis draws on full-text reading and contextual integration.

Representative examples:
- "88%" for occupational diseases (Roquelaure_2011): this is a well-known French national statistic, likely from the introduction/background of the cited article rather than its primary finding.
- "240,000 patients" (Monrad_2018): this is a sample size descriptor, not a claim result.
- "tableau 57" (Roquelaure_2011): a regulatory reference, not a numerical finding.
- "7% to 26%" (Hill_2010, Hodgetts_2021, Lucas_2022): range synthesis across multiple studies — not extractable from a single abstract.

No hallucinated claims were identified — all UNVERIFIED claims are traceable to reasonable full-text or synthesis origins.

**Recommendation**: No corrective action needed. The UNVERIFIED status is inherent to abstract-only extraction. Add a note in the limitations section if not already present (it is).

---

### [F-FID-03] Context vs result conflation risk for "88% of recognized occupational diseases" — Major

**Detail**: Line 27 states: "musculoskeletal disorders account for 88% of recognized occupational diseases" citing Roquelaure_2011 and Hagenström_2026. The 88% figure is a national-level background statistic (from French social security reports), not a primary finding of either cited study. Roquelaure_2011 studied risk factors for rotator cuff syndrome in 3,710 workers; Hagenström_2026 is a German study on epidemiology of subacromial shoulder pain. Neither study likely produced this specific figure as its own result.

**Recommendation**: Replace the current citations with a direct reference to the institutional source (e.g., Assurance Maladie / CNAM reports), or add an explicit qualifier: "According to national occupational health statistics [ref], musculoskeletal disorders account for 88%..."

### [F-FID-04] Specific OR values marked UNVERIFIED without full-text verification — Minor

**Detail**: Several specific odds ratios in the review carry `<!-- UNVERIFIED -->` comments:
- Line 107: OR = 2.82 for acromion morphology (Morelli_2019)
- Line 131: OR = 1.49 for diabetes (Giri_2023), OR = 3.31 for metabolic syndrome (Leong_2019)
- Line 181: OR = 1.79 for obesity (Guo_2022)
- Line 183: OR = 9.52 for combined risk (Vopat_2016)

These are appropriately flagged as UNVERIFIED. Without full-text access, their accuracy cannot be confirmed from abstracts alone.

**Recommendation**: Retain the `<!-- UNVERIFIED -->` markers. If full-text verification is performed later, update or remove the markers accordingly.

### [F-FID-05] Grey literature gap — Major

**Detail**: The protocol specifies French data as a geographic priority. The review acknowledges (line 197, line 233) that grey literature from French institutional sources (Santé Publique France, ANSES, DARES, CNAM/Assurance Maladie) was not systematically captured. This is a known limitation pattern for database-only searches.

Key missing sources likely include:
- CNAM annual reports on occupational diseases (tableau 57 statistics)
- DARES studies on work conditions and musculoskeletal disorders
- Santé Publique France epidemiological bulletins

**Recommendation**: Document this gap explicitly as a limitation (already done at lines 197 and 233). For future iterations, consider manual addition of key institutional reports.

---

### [F-FID-06] Cosali cohort cited under multiple keys without explicit acknowledgment — Minor

**Detail**: The Pays de la Loire Cosali cohort is cited under 5 different publication keys (Roquelaure_2011, Bodin_2012a, Bodin_2012b, Bodin_2012c, Bodin_2018) without explicit note that these represent analyses from the same underlying cohort. Line 93 mentions the cohort but does not flag the overlap.

**Recommendation**: Add a brief note in the Results or Discussion that these 5 studies draw on the same cohort, which means their findings are not fully independent.
