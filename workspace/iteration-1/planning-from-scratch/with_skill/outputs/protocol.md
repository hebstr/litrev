# Protocol — Systematic Review

## Research Question

In adults with prior Clostridioides difficile infection (CDI), are probiotics effective in preventing recurrent CDI compared to placebo or standard care?

## Framework: PICO

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **P** (Population) | Adults (>= 18 years) with a history of at least one *Clostridioides difficile* infection |
| **I** (Intervention) | Probiotics (any strain, formulation, dose, duration) administered as adjunctive or standalone therapy |
| **C** (Comparison) | Placebo, no probiotic, or standard of care alone (e.g., antibiotic therapy without probiotic supplementation) |
| **O** (Outcome) | Recurrence of CDI (primary); time to recurrence, adverse events, all-cause mortality (secondary) |

## Review Type

Systematic review

## Scope

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Time period | 2000--2026 (March) |
| Geographic scope | No restriction |
| Study designs | Randomized controlled trials (RCTs), quasi-experimental studies, prospective cohort studies with a comparator arm |
| Language | English, French |

## Inclusion Criteria

- Studies evaluating probiotics for prevention of recurrent CDI
- Population: adults (>= 18 years) with >= 1 prior CDI episode
- Comparator arm present (placebo, no probiotic, or standard care)
- Outcome reported: CDI recurrence rate
- Study designs: RCTs, quasi-experimental, prospective comparative cohorts
- Published 2000--2026
- English or French language
- Peer-reviewed publications and registered trial results

## Exclusion Criteria

- Studies focused solely on primary CDI prevention (no prior episode)
- Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) as the sole intervention
- Animal or in-vitro studies
- Case reports, case series without comparator, editorials, opinion pieces, conference abstracts without full data
- Pediatric-only populations (< 18 years)
- Studies where probiotic was a minor component of a complex bundled intervention and its effect cannot be isolated
