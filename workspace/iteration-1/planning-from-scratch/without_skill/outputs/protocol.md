# Systematic Review Protocol: Efficacy of Probiotics in Preventing Recurrent Clostridioides difficile Infection

## 1. Research Question

**Primary question:** Among adults who have experienced at least one episode of Clostridioides difficile infection (CDI), does adjunctive probiotic administration (compared to standard antibiotic therapy alone or placebo) reduce the risk of recurrent CDI?

**Secondary questions:**
- Which probiotic strains or combinations demonstrate the strongest evidence for preventing CDI recurrence?
- Does the timing and duration of probiotic administration influence recurrence rates?
- What is the safety profile of probiotics in this population, including adverse events and the rare risk of probiotic-related bacteremia/fungemia?

## 2. Framework: PICO

| Element | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Population** | Adults (>=18 years) with a confirmed diagnosis of CDI (toxin assay or NAAT-positive) who have completed or are undergoing standard-of-care antibiotic treatment (vancomycin, fidaxomicin, or metronidazole). Includes first recurrence, multiple recurrences, and initial episode populations (subgroup analysis). Excludes immunocompromised patients requiring ICU-level care (separate subgroup if data allow). |
| **Intervention** | Administration of probiotics (any species, strain, dose, formulation) as adjunct to standard antibiotic therapy. Key strains of interest: *Saccharomyces boulardii*, *Lactobacillus* spp., *Bifidobacterium* spp., multi-strain preparations. |
| **Comparator** | Placebo, no probiotic supplementation, or standard antibiotic therapy alone. |
| **Outcomes** | **Primary:** Recurrence of CDI within 8 weeks of completing treatment. **Secondary:** Time to recurrence; all-cause mortality at 30 and 90 days; adverse events (GI symptoms, probiotic-related bloodstream infections); quality of life measures; duration of diarrhea; healthcare utilization (hospitalization, length of stay). |

## 3. Scope

- **Study designs included:** Randomized controlled trials (RCTs), quasi-randomized trials, and prospective controlled cohort studies. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses will be used for cross-referencing but not included as primary evidence.
- **Publication period:** Inception to March 2026.
- **Languages:** English, French. Non-English/French studies with English abstracts will be screened; full texts will be evaluated if translation is feasible.
- **Geographic scope:** No restriction.
- **Setting:** Inpatient, outpatient, and long-term care facilities.
- **Exclusion of populations:** Pediatric patients (<18 years), patients receiving fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) as the primary intervention (FMT studies may be referenced in the discussion but are outside scope).

## 4. Search Strategy

### 4.1 Databases

- PubMed / MEDLINE
- Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL)
- Embase
- Web of Science Core Collection
- ClinicalTrials.gov (for ongoing/unpublished trials)
- WHO International Clinical Trials Registry Platform (ICTRP)

### 4.2 Search Terms

The search will combine three concept blocks using Boolean operators.

**Block 1 -- Population (C. difficile infection)**
```
("Clostridioides difficile" OR "Clostridium difficile" OR "C. difficile"
OR "CDI" OR "CDAD" OR "Clostridioides difficile infection"
OR "pseudomembranous colitis")
```

**Block 2 -- Intervention (Probiotics)**
```
("probiotic*" OR "Saccharomyces boulardii" OR "Lactobacillus"
OR "Bifidobacterium" OR "Lactocaseibacillus" OR "Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus"
OR "live biotherapeutic*" OR "microbial supplementation"
OR "beneficial bacteria" OR "synbiotic*")
```

**Block 3 -- Outcome (Recurrence / Prevention)**
```
("recurrence" OR "recurrent" OR "relapse" OR "reinfection"
OR "prevention" OR "prophylaxis" OR "secondary prevention"
OR "repeat episode*")
```

**Combined:** Block 1 AND Block 2 AND Block 3

Filters: Human studies only. No date restriction. Language filter: English, French.

### 4.3 Supplementary Search

- Hand-searching reference lists of included studies and relevant systematic reviews.
- Forward citation tracking of key RCTs (e.g., Surawicz et al. 2000, Allen et al. 2013, PLACIDE trial).
- Contact with authors of registered but unpublished trials if identified.
- Grey literature: conference abstracts from IDWeek, ECCMID, DDW (last 5 years).

## 5. Eligibility Criteria

### 5.1 Inclusion Criteria

1. Study evaluates probiotic administration in adults with confirmed CDI.
2. Comparator is placebo, no intervention, or standard care alone.
3. Reports at least one predefined outcome (recurrence rate as minimum).
4. RCT, quasi-RCT, or prospective controlled cohort design.
5. Full-text available or obtainable.

### 5.2 Exclusion Criteria

1. Studies where FMT is the primary intervention.
2. In vitro or animal studies.
3. Case reports, case series, narrative reviews, editorials, letters without original data.
4. Studies exclusively in pediatric populations.
5. Studies where probiotic is used for primary prevention of initial CDI episode (not recurrence) -- unless subgroup data on recurrence are reported separately.
6. Duplicate publications (most complete report retained).

## 6. Study Selection Process

1. **Deduplication** of search results using reference management software (Zotero).
2. **Title/abstract screening** by two independent reviewers against eligibility criteria. Conflicts resolved by discussion or third reviewer.
3. **Full-text screening** of retained records by two independent reviewers. Reasons for exclusion documented at this stage.
4. **PRISMA flow diagram** generated to report study selection.

## 7. Data Extraction

A standardized extraction form will capture:
- Study identifiers (author, year, country, setting, trial registration number)
- Study design and risk of bias domains
- Population characteristics (sample size, age, sex, CDI episode number, severity, comorbidities, immunosuppression status)
- Intervention details (strain, dose, formulation, timing of initiation relative to antibiotic therapy, duration)
- Comparator details
- Outcome data (events/total for dichotomous; mean/SD for continuous; time-to-event data if available)
- Funding source and conflicts of interest

## 8. Risk of Bias Assessment

- **RCTs:** Cochrane Risk of Bias tool (RoB 2).
- **Non-randomized studies:** ROBINS-I tool.
- Assessment performed independently by two reviewers; discrepancies resolved by consensus.

## 9. Data Synthesis

- **Qualitative synthesis:** Narrative summary of all included studies with evidence tables.
- **Quantitative synthesis (meta-analysis):** If >=2 studies are sufficiently homogeneous (population, intervention, outcome definition), random-effects meta-analysis (DerSimonian-Laird or restricted maximum likelihood) will be performed.
  - Effect measure: Risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI for dichotomous outcomes; mean difference (MD) or standardized mean difference (SMD) for continuous outcomes.
  - Heterogeneity assessed by Cochran's Q test and I-squared statistic (I-squared >50% considered substantial).
- **Subgroup analyses planned:**
  - By probiotic strain/species
  - By CDI episode number (first recurrence vs. multiple recurrences)
  - By timing of probiotic initiation (concurrent with antibiotics vs. post-antibiotic)
  - By patient setting (inpatient vs. outpatient)
- **Sensitivity analyses:** Excluding high risk-of-bias studies; excluding quasi-RCTs; excluding industry-funded studies.
- **Publication bias:** Funnel plot and Egger's test if >=10 studies included.
- **Certainty of evidence:** GRADE framework applied to each outcome across studies.

## 10. Software

- Reference management: Zotero
- Screening: Rayyan or Covidence
- Statistical analysis: R (meta, metafor packages)
- Reporting: Quarto

## 11. Registration and Reporting

- Protocol to be registered on PROSPERO prior to screening.
- Final report to follow PRISMA 2020 guidelines.
