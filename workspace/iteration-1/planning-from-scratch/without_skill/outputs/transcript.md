# Phase 1 Planning Transcript: Probiotics for Recurrent C. difficile

## Task

Systematic review on the efficacy of probiotics in preventing recurrent Clostridioides difficile infection.

## Approach

Phase 1 planning was performed without invoking any specialized skill. The protocol was constructed manually, covering all standard components of a systematic review protocol.

## Decisions Made

### 1. Research Question

Framed around whether adjunctive probiotics reduce recurrence of CDI in adults who have had at least one episode. Three secondary questions address strain-specific efficacy, timing/duration, and safety.

### 2. Framework Selection

**PICO** was chosen as the appropriate framework because this is an intervention-focused systematic review (treatment efficacy question). PEO or PCC would be more appropriate for qualitative or scoping reviews respectively.

### 3. Scope

- **Population:** Adults >=18 with confirmed CDI. FMT recipients and pediatric patients excluded.
- **Study designs:** RCTs, quasi-RCTs, prospective controlled cohorts. No case reports or narrative reviews.
- **Time:** No start date restriction (inception to March 2026).
- **Languages:** English and French.
- **Setting:** No geographic or healthcare-setting restriction.

### 4. Search Strategy

Three-block Boolean strategy combining C. difficile terms, probiotic terms, and recurrence/prevention terms. Six databases targeted: PubMed, CENTRAL, Embase, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov, and WHO ICTRP. Supplementary searching via reference list hand-searching, forward citation tracking, and grey literature from major infectious disease and gastroenterology conferences.

### 5. Eligibility Criteria

Inclusion requires a probiotic intervention, a comparator arm, and reporting of CDI recurrence. Exclusion covers FMT-only studies, preclinical work, case reports, pediatric-only populations, and studies on primary CDI prevention (unless recurrence subgroup data are available).

### 6. Protocol Structure

The full protocol includes 13 sections: research question, PICO framework, scope, search strategy, eligibility criteria, study selection process, data extraction plan, risk of bias assessment (RoB 2 for RCTs, ROBINS-I for non-randomized), data synthesis plan (random-effects meta-analysis, subgroup and sensitivity analyses, GRADE), software, registration/reporting, timeline, and amendment policy.
